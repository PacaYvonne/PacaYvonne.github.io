Why traditional "cleaning" doesn't help

You vacuum. You wash your sheets. You air out your room. But dust allergens are stubborn. Here's why normal routines fall short:

  • They’re microscopic: A single gram of household dust can contain thousands of allergen particles invisible to the eye.
  • They cling to fibers: Even frequent washing can’t fully remove allergenic proteins trapped deep in fabrics.
  • They multiply quickly: Each dust mite lives only a few weeks, but leaves behind hundreds of allergenic particles that linger for months.
