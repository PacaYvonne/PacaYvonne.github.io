The One Home Decor Trick Designers Swear By — And It Costs Less Than $50

By Claire Montgomery
Share:
Published on Aug 2, 2025

Tired of your home feeling “meh”? Here’s the simple switch that can completely transform a room in minutes.

You don’t need a renovation. You don’t need an interior designer. You just need… better lighting.

“Lighting is the single most overlooked part of decorating — but it’s also the most powerful,” says Laura Jenkins, a Los Angeles-based home stylist.

Here’s how to use lighting to take your space from dull to dazzling — no electrician required.

1. Ditch Overhead Lights — Use Layers Instead

Ceiling lights often cast harsh shadows and make a room feel flat. Instead, use lamps, sconces, and ambient lighting at different heights to add warmth and dimension.

Try this: Swap your bedside lamp for a warm LED sconce, or add a dimmable floor lamp in the corner of your living room.

2. Change Your Bulbs, Change Your Mood

Yes — the bulbs matter. Bright white bulbs make a space feel sterile (like a hospital). Warm-toned bulbs (around 2700K) add coziness and glow.

Best part? Bulbs are cheap. And changing them can make your space feel like a luxury hotel suite.

3. Add Statement Lighting

A funky pendant light or sculptural lamp can double as decor. Think of lighting as jewelry for your home — it’s where personality meets function.

Bonus: Lighting draws the eye. A statement piece can instantly elevate even the simplest space.

4. Don’t Forget the “Glow Zones”

Want your home to feel like a vibe? Light areas you normally ignore — like bookshelves, behind the TV, or under kitchen cabinets — with soft LED strips or puck lights.

These subtle touches create depth and ambiance without screaming for attention.

Before You Buy Another Throw Pillow…

Start with lighting. It sets the mood, adds style, and makes your home feel intentional. You’ll be surprised how big of a difference a few smart swaps can make — even if your budget’s under $50.
